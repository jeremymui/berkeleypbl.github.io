About Us

Our Mission

Future Business Leaders of America–Phi Beta Lambda is the largest student business organization in the nation. The UC Berkeley chapter of FBLA–PBL aims to develop well-rounded business leaders who care about making an impact in the world. To this end goal, we serve these three stages of a member’s personal and professional development:

Find Your Passion

Hone Your Skills

Build a Winning Profile

PBL at Berkeley strives to provide members with a comprehensive and dynamic learning experience, building the necessary technical and interpersonal skillset to succeed in wide range of business pursuits and intersections before gaining hands-on professional experience through our various consulting projects.

Our Values

You Get What You Put In

PBL members do not follow predetermined paths. Rather, we encourage you to define your own success, and how you achieve your goals. By providing you with the tools you need and endorsing a hands-on approach to learning, we enable you to own your projects. We want you to become leaders who think independently, inspired to maximize your potential. In PBL, you get what you put in, and our achievements are proof of that philosophy.

Small-Club Feel

At its core, PBL is not a business, nor a non-profit organization. We understand that, while preparing for life after college is important, our short 4 years here are just as significant and precious. And PBL’s member-centric mission reflects that. Joining PBL isn’t merely being a member of another on-campus organization, but becoming part of a family. We may come together from different backgrounds, but we unite to celebrate each other's successes, learn from each other's mistakes, and push each other to achieve more.

Question the Status Quo

We champion bold ideas. PBL holds a culture of innovation in high esteem, encouraging members to make an impact in both the organization and the real world. We not only welcome good ideas, but also complement them with our network and resources to help you turn your goals into reality. We attract people who challenge existing practices and constantly consider how to contribute and improve—people who go on together to leave an impact wherever they go.

Leave Your Legacy

PBL fosters leaders who leave legacies. Given our fleeting time in college, we seek to inspire each other to leave a mark on our committees, the organization, and UC Berkeley as a whole. We strive for more than temporary solutions that cease when you leave. Instead, we choose to focus our efforts on leading projects with long-lasting impact, so we can walk away knowing we’ve made a concrete, sustainable difference.

Our Community

Previous Next

With an intimate organizational structure and a semester filled with exciting social activities and outings, PBL's community is a family of diverse personalities and passions. Through a Big-Little mentorship program, social events planned by our Internal Networking committee, and our semesterly Member's Retreat, PBL welcomes its new members warmly while encouraging them to succeed professionally and personally.

Club Structure

Five project teams, known as committees, focus on different fields and intersections of business and pursue engaging and challenging curriculums to foster well-rounded members. In addition, PBL offers its consulting services to a wide range of clients from budding start-ups to Fortune 500 firms through its Upper Division client teams. Apart from projects completed within committees, members also experience the Internal Curriculum, an engaging series of professional development workshops dedicated to preparing members for a successful career, and participate in mentorship programs aimed at providing specific guidance for members' interests.

Consulting

The Consulting Committee aims to educate members through exposure to real life case studies. Honing their analytical skills and developing innovative strategies to solve a variety of business problems, members are prepared for a career in consulting, mastering project management techniques and insightful frameworks.

Finance

The Finance Committee provides knowledge of the basic principles of finance and a thorough understanding of the finance industry. Members will leave with reliable skills needed to not only ace technical finance interviews but also succeed in future finance-related work experiences.

Marketing

The Marketing Committee dives head-first into the intersection between business and the market with a comprehensive series of case studies. Learning to analyze, predict, and shape consumer reactions across specific industry landscapes, members will develop a sense of business creativity and the strategy ideation skills to suppport their acumen.

Technology

The Tech Committee explores and bridges the gap between technology and business. Members will not only advance their programming skills needed for data analysis and web development but will also gain a holistic view of how these skills are integrated into business.

Visual Media

The Visual Media Committee combines photography, graphic design, and cinematography in a comprehensive curriculum that explores the intersection between business and creative media. From exploring the technical side of branding to building and refining fundamental design skills, members will develop a marketable skillset of each art form.

Upper Division

The Upper Division Committee puts the technical skills garnered from the industry-specific committees into practice, taking on a diverse set of consulting projects. With an emphasis on impactful, hands-on engagement, the Upper Division project teams have worked with countless clients ranging from budding start-ups to Fortune 500 firms.
